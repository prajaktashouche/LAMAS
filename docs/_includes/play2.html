Description:

First, the cards are dealt. Alice is given an A♠ and a 3♥, Bob is given a 2♠ and a 3♠. Charlie is given a A♥ and a 2♥.
Alice places down Ace (A♠) and truthfully announces she has put down an Ace . If we denote Bob could doubt, however neither of his cards are Aces, so he believes it would be logical not to. Charlie could also doubt, since he has one Ace (A♥), however he does not know whether it is Alice or Bob who has the other Ace, so he waits. As neither has doubted, Bob can now infer that Charlie does not have 2 Aces, and Charlie can infer that Bob does not have 2 aces.
Bob places down 2 (2♠) and truthfully announces he has put down a 2 . Alice chooses not to doubt, as she does not own any 2s. Charlie does not doubt either, as he does not have both 2s. But now, again, as neither of them has doubted, Alice and Charlie both know that the other does not have both 2s.
Charlie must now place down a 3. However, he does not have any, instead of lying as he did in Play1 he decides to pass this turn.
It is Alice's turn now to place a 3. Since her final card is a 3 (3♥), she truthfully places the card and wins the round. In the final state of the graph we can see both Bob and Charlie has relation to possible worlds where Alice has a 3 in her hand and thus believes her statement to be true and does not doubt.

For Example: Bob has access to w55 from w0 and Charlie has access to w52 from w0. Both these worlds has Alice with a 3 in her hand.
